# Downloaded Papers

1. **When Thinking LLMs Lie: Unveiling the Strategic Deception in Representations of Reasoning Models** (2506.04909)
   - Authors: Kai Wang, Yihao Zhang, Meng Sun (2025)
   - Why relevant: Defines "strategic deception" and uses activation engineering to detect/control it in CoT models.

2. **Truth is Universal: Robust Detection of Lies in LLMs** (2407.12831)
   - Authors: Lennart Bürger, Fred A. Hamprecht, Boaz Nadler (2024)
   - Why relevant: Discovers a robust 2D truth subspace that generalizes across models and negations.

3. **Representation Engineering: A Top-Down Approach to AI Transparency** (2310.01405)
   - Authors: Andy Zou et al. (2023)
   - Why relevant: Foundational work for activation-based probing and steering.

4. **How to Catch an AI Liar: Lie Detection in Black-Box LLMs by Asking Unrelated Questions** (2309.15840)
   - Authors: Lorenzo Pacchiardi et al. (2023)
   - Why relevant: Provides a black-box baseline using follow-up questions.

5. **Alignment for Honesty** (2312.07000)
   - Authors: Yuqing Yang et al. (2023)
   - Why relevant: Discusses honesty steering and knowledge boundaries.
